Abstract#==#We present a generalized discriminative model for spelling error correction
which targets character-level transformations. While operating at the character
level, the model makes use of word-level and contextual information. In
contrast to previous work, the proposed approach learns to correct a variety of
error types without guidance of manually-selected constraints or
language-specific features. We apply the model to correct errors in Egyptian
Arabic dialect text, achieving 65\% reduction in word error rate over the input
baseline, and improving over the earlier state-of-the-art system.
