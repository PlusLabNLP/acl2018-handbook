Abstract#==#This paper introduces a new technique for phrase-structure
parser analysis, categorizing possible treebank structures by
integrating regular expressions into derivation trees.
We analyze the performance of the Berkeley parser on  OntoNotes WSJ
 and the English Web Treebank.                                                  
This provides some insight into the
evalb scores, and the problem of domain adaptation
with the web data.  We also analyze a ``test-on-train'' dataset,
showing a wide variance in how the parser is generalizing from
different structures  in the training material.
